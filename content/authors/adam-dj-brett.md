bio: Adam D. J. Brett is Visiting Professor for Digital Humanities and
  Indigenous Studies at United Lutheran Seminary and an International Research
  Associate with the American Indian Law Alliance. He also serves as an
  instructor at Syracuse University. His research focuses on the history of
  religions, Indigenous studies, and the intersections of religion, media, and
  digital humanities, with particular attention to the Doctrine of Discovery and
  its ongoing legal, theological, and cultural impacts. Brett's scholarship
  combines digital humanities methods with collaborative work alongside
  Indigenous communities and organizations. From 2022-2025 he was the grant &
  event coordinator for the Doctrine of Discovery Project at Syracuse University
  with Professor Philip P. Arnold, who is the Principal Investigator. The
  project is funded in part by the Henry Luce Grant "200 Years of Johnson v.
  McIntosh."  Professionally Adam has worked as a web developer for over a
  decade. He enjoys projects that allow him to integrate the academic study of
  religion with contemporary issues and web development.
