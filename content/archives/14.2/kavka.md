abstract: "An essay is presented on the philosophy of religion (PoR) with regard to two orientations to the subject. These orientations are the claim that the PoR's purpose is to promote a set of absolute moral norms and the claim that the field aims to determine whether religious communities' practices are true. Also explored are the tendency of the PoR to enable a thinking subject to make true claims, the mashup PoR, and the canon of modern PoR."
